# Uncovering the Secret Stacks of Indie Launches 🚀
 *_What tech are founders using to build and launch their products?_* 

Hey there! I'm Karan, and today I want to talk about something that caught my attention on Hacker News. 🤔 It's all about a new tool called StackScope, which aims to uncover the tech stacks behind over 40,000 indie product launches. 

## The Problem with Existing Stack Detection
We've all been there - trying to figure out what tech a particular website or product is using. There are already some existing tools that attempt to do this, but they often look at the web as a whole, rather than focusing on the current indie launch scene. This is where StackScope comes in - it specifically focuses on new product launches, scraping sites like Product Hunt, Show HN, and PeerPush to see what they're built with. 

### What StackScope Reveals
So, what kind of insights can we gain from StackScope? It watches launches and crawls the public site behind each one, looking for clues like hosting, frameworks, analytics, DNS, security headers, legal pages, and even AI-builder signals. This gives us a unique glimpse into what people are actually using to build and launch their products right now. 

## Implementation Details
I was curious about how StackScope was built, and it turns out it runs on .NET and uses Playwright for rendered pages. It also has a first-party fingerprint catalogue, rather than relying on data from Wappalyzer or other similar services. This approach allows for more accurate and up-to-date information. 

## My Take
As someone who's interested in the tech behind new product launches, I think StackScope is a game-changer. It's not just about satisfying our curiosity - it can also give us insights into trends and patterns in the indie launch scene. By seeing what others are using, we can make more informed decisions about our own tech stacks. 

## Conclusion
StackScope is an exciting new tool that sheds light on the secret stacks of indie launches. Whether you're a founder, developer, or just a tech enthusiast, it's definitely worth checking out. So, go ahead and explore StackScope to see what you can discover.
