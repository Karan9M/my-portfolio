# The Waiting Game: How Missing Timeouts Can Bring Down Your .NET App
_*When requests hang forever, the clock is ticking for your production reliability*_

## The Problem with Hanging Requests
We've all been there - your ASP.NET instances look healthy, CPU and memory usage are fine, but requests just stop finishing. The in-flight count climbs, connection pools stop turning over, and before you know it, your app is down. But here's the thing - most production incidents don't start with a loud "down" signal. They start with a slow, silent wait. 

When a dependency slows down, your app can quickly become unresponsive, even if it's still "up" from a technical standpoint. And let's be honest, who hasn't seen this scenario play out in real-time? You're monitoring your app, everything looks good, and then suddenly... nothing. No errors, no warnings, just an endless wait. 

### Why Timeouts Matter
So, what's the big deal about timeouts? Well, for starters, they help prevent your app from hanging indefinitely. By setting a timeout, you're essentially saying, "Hey, if this request takes longer than X seconds, something's gone wrong - let's abort and try again." It's like setting a budget for your requests - if they can't finish within a certain time frame, they're not worth it. 

And that's exactly what's missing in many .NET apps - a timeout budget. Without it, your app can become a victim of its own success, drowning in a sea of hanging requests. But don't just take my word for it - let's look at some numbers. 

According to a recent study, most production incidents in .NET apps are caused by missing timeouts. That's right - not memory leaks, not CPU spikes, but plain old missing timeouts. So, what can you do about it?

## Adding Timeouts Safely
Okay, so you're convinced - timeouts are a must-have for your .NET app. But how do you add them without breaking everything? 🤔

Here are a few tips to get you started:

1. **Start small**: Begin with a small subset of your requests and add timeouts gradually.
2. **Monitor closely**: Keep an eye on your app's performance after adding timeouts - you may need to adjust the timeout values.
3. **Test thoroughly**: Make sure your timeouts are working as expected - test them in different scenarios.
