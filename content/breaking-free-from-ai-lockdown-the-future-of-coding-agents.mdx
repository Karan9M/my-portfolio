# Breaking Free from AI Lockdown: The Future of Coding Agents 🚀
_*Unlocking the true potential of LLMs, one pipeline at a time*_

Hey there! I'm Karan, and today I want to talk about something that's been bugging me for a while now. 🤔 The way AI coding tools are designed is quite limiting, and I'm not the only one who thinks so. 

## The Problem with Current AI Coding Tools
We've all been there - you sign up for an AI coding tool, agree to a per-seat subscription, and get stuck with a single model that the vendor picked for you. 📈 But what if you want to swap models or try out a different one? You're out of luck because the whole thing is glued to the vendor's SDK. 

This approach locks users out of one of the most valuable properties of Large Language Models (LLMs): they are swappable, comparable, and increasingly cheap. 📊 It's like being stuck with a single camera lens when you could be experimenting with different ones to find the perfect shot. 

## Introducing Anvil: The Game-Changer
So, what's the solution? 🤔 Enter Anvil, an open-source coding agent that takes a one-line feature request and ships a PR end-to-end. 🚀 The thing that makes it different is that it routes each pipeline stage to a different LLM, giving you the freedom to experiment and find the best model for your needs. 

### How Anvil Works
Anvil is designed to be flexible and adaptable. It allows you to swap out models, compare results, and even add your own custom models to the pipeline. 🤖 This means you can try out different LLMs, see which one works best for your project, and make changes on the fly. 

### Why This Matters to Developers
1. **It saves you time** — and time is money, friend. 🕒 With Anvil, you can automate repetitive tasks and focus on what matters most - writing code. 
2. **It's surprisingly easy to pick up** — even if you're new to AI coding tools, Anvil's intuitive interface makes it easy to get started. 
3. **You're not locked in** — with Anvil, you have the freedom to choose the models you want to use, and even add your own custom models to the pipeline.
