# When Updates Go Wrong: A Cautionary Tale of Tauri 2.0 🚨
*_A bug, a crash, and a whole lot of lessons learned_* 

Hey there! I'm Karan, and today I want to talk about something that's all too familiar to us developers: the dreaded update bug 🤦. We've all been there - you push out a new version of your app, and suddenly, everything that can go wrong does. That's exactly what happened to the team behind a popular desktop app, which uses Tauri 2.0 to deliver offline-first project management tools to small teams.

## The Incident: A 3-Hour Nightmare
On October 17, 2024, the team deployed a routine update to their app, which is used by over 10,000 active monthly users. Within 12 minutes, they saw a 100% error rate for app launch, with users unable to access core functionality. The incident lasted 3 hours and 7 minutes, fully resolved by 17:29 UTC. But what caused this chaos?

### Root Cause: A Patch Gone Wrong
The October 17 update included a patch for Tauri's core IPC handler. Unfortunately, this patch introduced a bug that broke the app's functionality. The team's postmortem analysis reveals that the issue was caused by a faulty update process, which highlights the importance of thorough testing before deploying updates to production.

## My Take
As a developer, I've seen my fair share of update bugs. But what I find really interesting about this incident is how the team responded to it. They were transparent about the issue, communicated clearly with their users, and took concrete steps to prevent similar incidents in the future. That's the kind of attitude we should all strive for when things go wrong.

### What We Can Learn
So, what can we take away from this incident? Firstly, testing is key. Before deploying any update, make sure you've tested it thoroughly to catch any potential bugs. Secondly, communication is essential. When things go wrong, be transparent with your users, and keep them informed about what's happening. Finally, learn from your mistakes. Take the time to analyze what went wrong and implement changes to prevent similar incidents in the future.

## Conclusion
In conclusion, the Tauri 2.0 update bug that broke the desktop app for 10,000 users is a cautionary tale that reminds us of the importance of testing, communication, and learning from our mistakes. As developers, we can all learn from this incident and strive to do better in the future.
TL;DR: When updates go wrong, it's essential to be transparent, communicate clearly, and learn from your mistakes. Start by testing thoroughly, and always keep your users informed 🚀.
